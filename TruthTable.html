Variables/Operators Syntax
Literals A-Z, a-z
TRUE 1
FALSE 0
NOT !
AND &
OR |
XOR ^

Only the above four logical operators are available; if other logic gates like XNOR, NOR, NAND, are needed, conversion is necessary (refer to cheatsheet). AND<&> will take precedence over the other logic gates; use parenthesis<()> to avoid ambiguous expressions. Only supports up to 5 unique variables.

eg. To generate the truth table for
(A AND B) OR (1 XOR C')'
The input should be
(A & B) | !(1 ^ !C)

Note: the input validation may not account for all errors, please ensure the syntax of the input is correct.

Operator Example Converted Form
NAND A NAND B !(A & B)
NOR A NOR B !(A | B)
XNOR A XNOR B !(A ^ B)
